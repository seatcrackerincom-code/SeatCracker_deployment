SEATCRACKER ROADMAP GENERATION MECHANISM
=========================================

This document explains how the "SeatCracker" roadmap system calculates your study plan.

1. SMART MODE (AI GENERATION)
-----------------------------
Mechanism: LLM-Powered Planning (Groq Cloud)
Model: Llama-3.3-70b-versatile

Process:
- Inputs: The system sends your exact available days, daily study hours, priority order (e.g., High -> Medium -> Low), and the FULL syllabus of your course to the AI.
- System Prompt: The AI is instructed as an "Expert EAMCET Study Planner."
- Rules Enforced:
    * Subject Balance: For Engineering, the AI is commanded to allocate 50% of time to Maths, 25% to Physics, and 25% to Chemistry.
    * Priority: It follows your selected priority order (e.g., if you select Medium first, it finishes all Medium topics before starting High).
    * Multi-Subject Days: Every day is forced to have at least 2 subjects to prevent burnout.
    * Full Coverage: It is restricted from skipping any topic in the list.

2. MANUAL MODE (LOCAL LOGIC)
----------------------------
Mechanism: Algorithmic Distribution (Client-side JS)

Process:
- Sorting: The system takes only the topics you manually checked. It sorts them based on your "Priority Order Selection" (High, Medium, or Low).
- Subject Ratios: 
    * Engineering: Mathematics (0.5), Physics (0.25), Chemistry (0.25).
    * Agriculture/Pharmacy: All subjects equal (0.25 each).
- Distribution: 
    * It calculates the total minutes available per day (Daily Hours * 60).
    * It uses the ratios to determine how many minutes each subject gets per day.
    * It then "pulls" topics from your sorted list until that subject's daily time is filled.
    * Topics are interleaved so you study multiple subjects daily.

3. TIME CALCULATION (HOW Topic Duration IS DECIDED)
--------------------------------------------------
The system doesn't just guess; it calculates "Topic Density":

A. In AI Mode:
- The AI looks at the total topics vs. total days. 
- If you have 6 hours/day, it assigns more topics per day with shorter durations (e.g., 1.5h per topic).
- If you have 2 hours/day, it stretches topics across more days or combines them into smaller 45m chunks.

B. In Manual Mode:
- Average Topic Base: 35 minutes.
- Scaling: If you increase "Daily Study Hours", the system increases the "Topics Per Day" count rather than making one topic 5 hours long.
- Formula: 
    topics_per_day = (Daily Minutes * Subject Ratio) / 35 minutes.
- Result: Each task is assigned a specific time string (e.g., "1h 10m") based on how many topics it needs to "squeeze" into that day's allocated subject slot.

4. DATES & DEADLINES
--------------------
- Exam Date: Hardcoded based on official 2024/25 trends (AP: May 12, TS: May 9).
- Study End Date: Set 5 days before the exam to allow for Mock Tests.
- Remaining Days: Calculated automatically as (Study End Date - Today).
