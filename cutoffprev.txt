IMPORTANT NOTES BEFORE READING:
---------------------------------
1. Total marks = 360 (from 2017 onwards mostly 360, varies by year)
2. TWO types of cutoffs exist every year:
   TYPE A = QUALIFYING CUTOFF    → minimum to be in rank list
   TYPE B = ADMISSION CUTOFF     → minimum rank to get IIT seat
   This file covers TYPE A (Qualifying Cutoff) — what students
   must score to even appear in the rank list.
3. Subject cutoff must be met in ALL THREE subjects individually
   PLUS the aggregate cutoff — BOTH conditions must be true.
4. GEN-EWS category was introduced only from 2019 onwards.
5. Cutoffs are LOWERED in difficult years, RAISED in easy years.

CATEGORY RELAXATION PATTERN — CONSISTENT ACROSS ALL YEARS
================================================================

The PERCENTAGE thresholds follow this pattern every year:

GEN (CRL)    = BASE (100%)
GEN-EWS      = 90% of GEN threshold  (10% relaxation)
OBC-NCL      = 90% of GEN threshold  (10% relaxation)
SC           = 50% of GEN threshold  (50% relaxation)
ST           = 50% of GEN threshold  (50% relaxation)
PwD (any)    = 50% of GEN threshold  (50% relaxation)
Preparatory  = 25% of GEN threshold  (75% relaxation)

EXAMPLE using 2024 (CRL = 30.34% aggregate):
  GEN-EWS  = 27.30%  (= 30.34% × 0.90)
  OBC-NCL  = 27.30%  (= 30.34% × 0.90)
  SC/ST    = 15.17%  (= 30.34% × 0.50)
  Prep     = 7.58%   (= 30.34% × 0.25)

This pattern has been CONSISTENT since 2013.
GEN-EWS was added in 2019 with same relaxation as OBC-NCL.

KEY TRENDS & ANALYSIS FOR SEATCRACKER
================================================================

TREND 1 — BIG FLUCTUATIONS ARE NORMAL:
  2022: CRL = 55/360 (15%) — one of the lowest ever
  2024: CRL = 109/360 (30%) — one of the highest ever
  Swing of 54 marks in just 2 years!
  Tell students: Don't target just cutoff — aim much higher.

TREND 2 — SUBJECT-WISE CUTOFF IS THE REAL TRAP:
  Many students score well in aggregate but fail ONE subject.
  They miss the rank list despite good total score.
  SeatCracker should highlight subject-wise danger clearly.

TREND 3 — PRE-2019 vs POST-2019:
  Pre-2019: Only CRL, OBC-NCL, SC, ST, PwD categories
  Post-2019: GEN-EWS added (economic reservation category)
  GEN-EWS gets same relaxation as OBC-NCL (10% below GEN)

TREND 4 — REVISED CUTOFFS (2018 and 2019):
  IIT can and does lower cutoffs after seeing results.
  2019: CRL original 35% revised to 25%
  2018: CRL original 35% revised to 25%
  This happens when paper difficulty is underestimated.

TREND 5 — COVID IMPACT (2020, 2021, 2022):
  Three consecutive low-cutoff years.
  Cutoffs returned to normal from 2023 onwards.

TREND 6 — TOTAL MARKS VARIATION:
  2015: 504 marks (highest ever)
  2020: 396 marks
  Most years: 360 marks
  Always use PERCENTAGE not absolute marks for comparison.

TREND 7 — ADMISSION CUTOFF vs QUALIFYING CUTOFF:
  Qualifying cutoff (this file) = just to enter rank list
  Actual admission to IIT Bombay CSE needs AIR < 100
  Real competition is far above these minimums!
