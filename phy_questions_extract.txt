■■ UNITS & MEASUREMENTS
Q01.
The dimensional formula of angular momentum is:
A) [ML²T■¹]
B) [MLT■¹]
C) [ML²T■²]
D) [ML³T■¹]
■ Answer: A) [ML²T■¹]
■ L = mvr = [M][LT■¹][L] = [ML²T■¹]. Also L = Iω = [ML²][T■¹] = [ML²T■¹]
Q02.
The percentage error in measurement of radius is 2%. The percentage error in volume of sphere is:
A) 2%
B) 4%
C) 6%
D) 8%
■ Answer: C) 6%
■ V = (4/3)πr³. % error in V = 3 × (% error in r) = 3 × 2% = 6%
Q03.
Which of the following pairs has the same dimensional formula?
A) Work and Power
B) Torque and Work
C) Linear momentum and Force
D) Stress and Velocity
■ Answer: B) Torque and Work
■ Torque τ = F×r = [ML²T■²]; Work W = F·d = [ML²T■²]. Same dimensions!
Q04.
Light year is a unit of:
A) Time
B) Speed
C) Distance
D) Intensity
■ Answer: C) Distance
■ 1 light year = distance light travels in one year ≈ 9.46 × 10¹■ m.
■■ KINEMATICS
Q05.
A body is thrown vertically upward with velocity u. Time to reach maximum height is:
A) u/g
B) 2u/g
C) u/2g
D) g/u
■ Answer: A) u/g
■ At max height, v = 0. Using v = u - gt → 0 = u - gt → t = u/g
Q06.
A projectile is fired at angle θ with horizontal. The horizontal range is maximum when θ equals:
A) 30°
B) 45°
C) 60°
D) 90°
■ Answer: B) 45°
■ R = u²sin2θ/g. R is maximum when sin2θ = 1 → 2θ = 90° → θ = 45°

Q07.
Velocity-time graph of uniform acceleration is a:
A) Parabola
B) Hyperbola
C) Straight line
D) Circle
■ Answer: C) Straight line
■ v = u + at. This is linear in t → v-t graph is a straight line with slope = a.
Q08.
A ball is dropped from height h. Time to reach ground is:
A) √(h/g)
B) √(2h/g)
C) 2√(h/g)
D) √(h/2g)
■ Answer: B) √(2h/g)
■ h = ½gt² → t = √(2h/g). Initial velocity u = 0 for a dropped body.
Q09.
For a particle moving in a circle of radius r with uniform speed v, centripetal acceleration is:
A) v²r
B) v/r²
C) v²/r
D) vr²
■ Answer: C) v²/r
■ Centripetal acceleration a = v²/r = ω²r. Directed towards centre.
■■ LAWS OF MOTION
Q10.
A body of mass 5 kg is acted upon by a net force of 20 N. Its acceleration is:
A) 2 m/s²
B) 4 m/s²
C) 10 m/s²
D) 100 m/s²
■ Answer: B) 4 m/s²
■ Newton's 2nd law: F = ma → a = F/m = 20/5 = 4 m/s²
Q11.
A body of mass 2 kg moving at 10 m/s collides and sticks to a body of mass 3 kg at rest. Their
combined velocity is:
A) 2 m/s
B) 4 m/s
C) 5 m/s
D) 6 m/s
■ Answer: B) 4 m/s
■ Conservation of momentum: m■u■ = (m■+m■)v → 2×10 = 5×v → v = 4 m/s
Q12.
The coefficient of static friction between a block and surface is 0.4 (g=10 m/s²). Maximum static friction
on a 5 kg block is:
A) 10 N
B) 15 N
C) 20 N
D) 25 N
■ Answer: C) 20 N
■ f_s(max) = µ■ × N = µ■ × mg = 0.4 × 5 × 10 = 20 N

Q13.
Which of the following is a consequence of Newton's Third Law?
A) Conservation of energy
B) Conservation of momentum
C) Conservation of charge
D) Conservation of angular momentum
■ Answer: B) Conservation of
momentum
■ Newton's 3rd law (action-reaction) leads directly to conservation of linear
momentum.
Q14.
A 60 kg person stands in a lift accelerating upward at 2 m/s². Apparent weight (g=10 m/s²) is:
A) 480 N
B) 600 N
C) 720 N
D) 840 N
■ Answer: C) 720 N
■ Apparent weight = m(g+a) = 60(10+2) = 60×12 = 720 N
■■ WORK, ENERGY & POWER
Q15.
The work done by a force F = 5 N on a body through displacement d = 4 m at 60° is:
A) 5 J
B) 10 J
C) 15 J
D) 20 J
■ Answer: B) 10 J
■ W = F·d·cosθ = 5 × 4 × cos60° = 5 × 4 × 0.5 = 10 J
Q16.
The kinetic energy of a body of mass 2 kg moving at 6 m/s is:
A) 12 J
B) 18 J
C) 36 J
D) 72 J
■ Answer: C) 36 J
■ KE = ½mv² = ½ × 2 × 6² = ½ × 2 × 36 = 36 J
Q17.
A spring of force constant k is compressed by x. The elastic potential energy stored is:
A) kx
B) kx²
C) ½kx²
D) 2kx²
■ Answer: C) ½kx²
■ Elastic PE = ½kx². This energy is released when spring returns to natural
length.
Q18.
A machine does 1000 J of work in 10 s. Its power output is:
A) 10 W
B) 100 W
C) 1000 W
D) 10000 W
■ Answer: B) 100 W
■ Power P = W/t = 1000/10 = 100 W

Q19.
In an elastic collision:
A) Only momentum is conserved
B) Only KE is conserved
C) Both momentum and KE are conserved
D) Neither is conserved
■ Answer: C) Both momentum
and KE are conserved
■ Elastic collision: both linear momentum AND kinetic energy are conserved.
Coefficient of restitution = 1.
■■ ROTATIONAL MOTION
Q20.
The moment of inertia of a solid disc of mass M and radius R about its central axis is:
A) MR²
B) ½MR²
C) ■MR²
D) ¼MR²
■ Answer: B) ½MR²
■ I(disc) = ½MR². For ring I = MR², for solid sphere I = 2/5 MR².
Q21.
Angular momentum L = Iω. If I is halved and angular momentum is conserved, ω becomes:
A) ω/2
B) ω
C) 2ω
D) 4ω
■ Answer: C) 2ω
■ L = Iω = constant. If I → I/2, then ω → 2ω (like ice skater pulling in arms).
Q22.
Torque τ = r × F. The SI unit of torque is:
A) Joule
B) Newton
C) Newton-metre
D) Watt
■ Answer: C) Newton-metre
■ τ = r × F → unit = m × N = N·m. Although dimensions equal Joule, torque is
expressed in N·m.
Q23.
A body rolls without slipping. The ratio of rotational KE to total KE for a solid sphere is:
A) 2/7
B) 5/7
C) 1/2
D) 2/5
■ Answer: A) 2/7
■ KE_rot/KE_total = (I/MR²)/(1 + I/MR²) = (2/5)/(1+2/5) = (2/5)/(7/5) = 2/7
Q24.
The theorem of parallel axes states that I = Icm + Md². Here d is:
A) Radius of body
B) Distance between the two parallel axes
C) Distance from centre of mass to surface
D) Diameter of the body
■ Answer: B) Distance between
the two parallel axes
■ Parallel axis theorem: I = Icm + Md² where d = distance between parallel axis
and CM axis.

■■ GRAVITATION

A) 5.9 km/s
B) 7.9 km/s
C) 11.2 km/s
D) 3.0 km/s
■ Answer: B) 7.9 km/s
■ v■ = √(gR) = √(9.8 × 6.4×10■) ≈ 7.9 km/s
Q26.
Kepler's third law states that T² ∝ r³. If the orbital radius is doubled, the time period becomes:
A) 2T
B) 4T
C) 2√2 T
D) √2 T
■ Answer: C) 2√2 T
■ T² ∝ r³ → (T'/T)² = (2r/r)³ = 8 → T' = T√8 = 2√2 T
Q27.
Escape velocity from Earth's surface is:
A) √(gR)
B) √(2gR)
C) 2√(gR)
D) gR
■ Answer: B) √(2gR)
■ v_esc = √(2GM/R) = √(2gR) ≈ 11.2 km/s for Earth.
Q28.
The gravitational potential energy of a body of mass m at height h above Earth (radius R) is:
A) -GMm/R
B) -GMm/(R+h)
C) GMm/(R+h)
D) mgh
■ Answer: B) -GMm/(R+h)
■ U = -GMm/r where r = R+h from Earth's centre. Negative sign: bound system.

B) Volumetric stress / Volumetric strain

Surface tension has dimensions:

B) [ML■¹T■²]

Q31.
Bernoulli's theorem is based on conservation of:
A) Mass
B) Momentum
C) Energy
D) Charge
■ Answer: C) Energy
■ Bernoulli's equation: P + ½ρv² + ρgh = constant → conservation of energy per
unit volume.
Q32.
The excess pressure inside a soap bubble of radius R and surface tension T is:
A) T/R
B) 2T/R
C) 4T/R
D) T/2R

■■ THERMAL PHYSICS
Q33.
For an ideal gas at temperature T, the rms speed of molecules is proportional to:
A) T
B) T²
C) √T
D) 1/√T
■ Answer: C) √T
■ v_rms = √(3RT/M) ∝ √T. Doubling T increases v_rms by factor √2.
Q34.
For a monoatomic ideal gas, the ratio γ = Cp/Cv is:
A) 1.4
B) 1.67
C) 1.33
D) 1.0
■ Answer: B) 1.67
■ Monoatomic gas: f=3. Cv = 3R/2, Cp = 5R/2. γ = Cp/Cv = 5/3 ≈ 1.67
Q35.
Newton's law of cooling states that the rate of cooling is proportional to:
A) Temperature of the body
B) Temperature of surroundings
C) Difference in temperatures of body and surroundings
D) Square of temperature difference
■ Answer: C) Difference in
temperatures of body and
surroundings
■ dQ/dt ∝ (T - T■). Rate of cooling depends on temperature excess over
surroundings.
Q36.
The efficiency of a Carnot engine operating between 300 K and 500 K is:
A) 30%
B) 40%
C) 60%
D) 50%
■ Answer: B) 40%
■ η = 1 - T■/T■ = 1 - 300/500 = 1 - 0.6 = 0.4 = 40%

Q37.
Wien's displacement law states that λ_max × T = constant. If T is doubled, λ_max:
A) Doubles
B) Halves
C) Quadruples
D) Remains same
■ Answer: B) Halves
■ λ_max ∝ 1/T. If T → 2T, then λ_max → λ_max/2.

Q38.
The time period of a simple pendulum of length L is:
A) T = 2π√(g/L)
B) T = 2π√(L/g)
C) T = π√(L/g)
D) T = √(2πL/g)
■ Answer: B) T = 2π√(L/g)
■ T = 2π√(L/g). Time period is independent of mass and amplitude (for small
oscillations).
Q39.
For SHM x = A sinωt, the maximum velocity is:
A) Aω²
B) A/ω
C) Aω
D) A²ω
■ Answer: C) Aω
■ v = dx/dt = Aω cosωt. Maximum when cosωt = 1 → v_max = Aω (at mean
position).
Q40.
In SHM, at mean position the restoring force is:
A) Maximum
B) Minimum
C) Zero
D) Constant
■ Answer: C) Zero
■ F = -kx. At mean position x = 0 → F = 0. Velocity is maximum here.
Q41.
The time period of a spring-mass system (mass m, spring constant k) is:
A) 2π√(k/m)
B) 2π√(m/k)
C) π√(m/k)

■■ WAVES & SOUND
Q42.
The speed of sound in air at 0°C is 332 m/s. At 4°C it is approximately:
A) 328 m/s
B) 332 m/s
C) 333.2 m/s
D) 340 m/s
■ Answer: C) 333.2 m/s
■ v ∝ √T. v = 332√(277/273) ≈ 333.2 m/s. Speed of sound increases with
temperature.
Q43.
Doppler effect: A source moves towards a stationary observer. The observed frequency:
A) Decreases
B) Increases
C) Remains same
D) Becomes zero
■ Answer: B) Increases
■ When source approaches observer, wavefronts are compressed → shorter
wavelength → higher frequency.
Q44.
Beats are produced due to:
A) Interference of two waves of same frequency
B) Superposition of two waves of slightly different
frequencies
C) Diffraction of waves
D) Resonance
■ Answer: B) Superposition of
two waves of slightly different
frequencies
■ Beat frequency = |f■ - f■|. Beats are periodic variations in loudness due to
interference.
Q45.
For a standing wave, nodes are points of:
A) Maximum displacement
B) Zero displacement
C) Maximum velocity
D) Maximum pressure
■ Answer: B) Zero displacement
■ Nodes: zero displacement (max pressure variation). Antinodes: max
displacement (min pressure variation).
Q46.
The frequency of the fundamental mode of vibration of an open pipe of length L is:
A) v/4L
B) v/2L
C) v/L
D) 2v/L
■ Answer: B) v/2L
■ Open pipe: both ends antinodes. Fundamental: L = λ/2 → f = v/2L (closed:
v/4L).

■■ ELECTROSTATICS
Q47.
Coulomb's law: Force between charges q■ and q■ at distance r is F = kq■q■/r². If distance is tripled,

A) 3F
B) F/3
C) 9F
D) F/9
■ Answer: D) F/9
■ F ∝ 1/r². If r → 3r, F → F/9. Force decreases by factor 9.
Q48.
Electric potential at distance r from charge q is:
A) kq/r²
B) kq/r
C) kq·r
D) kq²/r
■ Answer: B) kq/r
■ V = kq/r. Electric field E = kq/r² (note: E ∝ 1/r², V ∝ 1/r).
Q49.
The capacitance of a parallel plate capacitor with area A, plate separation d and dielectric constant K is:
A) ε■A/d
B) Kε■A/d
C) ε■A/Kd

■ Answer: B) Kε■A/d
■ C = Kε■A/d. Dielectric constant K increases capacitance by factor K.
Q50.
Energy stored in a capacitor of capacitance C charged to voltage V is:
A) CV
B) ½CV²
C) CV²
D) C²V
■ Answer: B) ½CV²
■ U = ½CV² = ½QV = Q²/2C. All three expressions are equivalent.
Q51.
Gauss's law: For a spherical shell of charge Q, electric field inside is:
A) kQ/R²
B) kQ/r²
C) Zero
D) kQ/2R²
■ Answer: C) Zero
■ By Gauss's law, E inside a uniformly charged shell = 0 (no enclosed charge for r
< R).

Q55.
The Wheatstone bridge is balanced when:

Q56.
The power dissipated in a resistor R carrying current I is:

■■ MAGNETIC EFFECTS OF CURRENT

B) Weber

D) Gauss

Q63.

Q64.
At resonance in a series LCR circuit:

B) XL < XC

Q65.
Power factor of a purely inductive circuit is:
A) 1
B) 0.5
C) Zero
D) Infinity
■ Answer: C) Zero
■ PF = cosφ. For pure inductor, φ = 90° (current lags voltage). PF = cos90° = 0.
No real power.
Q66.
A transformer steps voltage from 220 V to 11000 V. If primary turns = 200, secondary turns are:
A) 1000
B) 4000
C) 10000
D) 40000
■ Answer: C) 10000
■ Vs/Vp = Ns/Np → 11000/220 = Ns/200 → Ns = 50 × 200 = 10000
■■ RAY & WAVE OPTICS
Q67.
The critical angle for total internal reflection for a medium of refractive index µ = √2 is:
A) 30°
B) 45°
C) 60°
D) 90°
■ Answer: B) 45°
■ sin(C) = 1/µ = 1/√2 → C = 45°. TIR occurs when angle of incidence > C.
Q68.
Mirror formula: 1/v + 1/u = 1/f. For a concave mirror of focal length 10 cm with object at 30 cm, image
distance is:
A) 10 cm
B) 15 cm
C) 20 cm
D) 30 cm
■ Answer: B) 15 cm
■ 1/v = 1/f - 1/u = 1/(-10) - 1/(-30) = -1/10 + 1/30 = -2/30 → v = -15 cm (real
image)
Q69.
In Young's double slit experiment, fringe width β = λD/d. If d is doubled (other things same), β:
A) Doubles
B) Halves
C) Quadruples
D) Remains same
■ Answer: B) Halves
■ β ∝ 1/d. If d → 2d, then β → β/2. Fringes become narrower.

Q70.
Polarization is a property of:
A) Longitudinal waves
B) Transverse waves
C) Both longitudinal and transverse
D) Sound waves
■ Answer: B) Transverse waves
■ Only transverse waves can be polarized. Light (transverse EM wave) can be
polarized; sound cannot.
Q71.
Resolving power of a microscope increases when:
A) Wavelength of light used increases
B) Wavelength of light used decreases
C) Aperture decreases
D) Magnification decreases
■ Answer: B) Wavelength of light
used decreases
■ RP = 2µsinθ/λ ∝ 1/λ. Smaller λ → better resolution. Electron microscopes use
very small λ.
Q72.
In a single slit diffraction pattern, the central maximum is how many times wider than secondary
maxima?
A) Same width
B) 2 times
C) 4 times
D) Half
■ Answer: B) 2 times
■ Central maximum width = 2λD/a; secondary maxima width = λD/a. Central is
twice as wide.
■■ MODERN PHYSICS
Q73.
In photoelectric effect, the stopping potential V■ is related to max KE of photoelectrons by:
A) KE_max = eV■
B) KE_max = eV■/2
C) KE_max = 2eV■
D) KE_max = e/V■
■ Answer: A) KE_max = eV■
■ Stopping potential V■ just stops fastest electrons. KE_max = eV■ (energy of

Q74.
Bohr's model: Radius of nth orbit of hydrogen is:
A) r = n²a■
B) r = na■
C) r = a■/n²
D) r = a■/n
■ Answer: A) r = n²a■
■ r■ = n²a■ where a■ = 0.529 Å (Bohr radius). r■ = 0.529 Å, r■ = 4 × 0.529 Å.
Q75.
In radioactive decay, the half-life T½ and decay constant λ are related by:
A) T½ = λ
B) T½ = 1/λ
C) T½ = 0.693/λ
D) T½ = λ/0.693
■ Answer: C) T½ = 0.693/λ
■ N = N■e^(-λt). At t = T½: N = N■/2 → e^(-λT½) = 1/2 → T½ = ln2/λ = 0.693/λ

Q76.
In nuclear fission, the energy released is due to:
